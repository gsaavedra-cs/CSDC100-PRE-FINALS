Frequently Asked Questions

Short answers to common questions about Generative AI (GenAI), its uses, limitations, and best practices.

1. What is Generative AI?

Generative AI (GenAI) refers to AI systems capable of producing new content — text, images, audio, video, and code — by learning patterns from large datasets. These systems are often used to accelerate creative work, assist with drafting, and prototype ideas quickly.

2. How does it work?

Most GenAI models are trained using large-scale deep learning (e.g., transformers) on diverse datasets. They learn to predict next tokens or pixels, and can be conditioned with prompts. Techniques like fine-tuning, instruction-tuning, and retrieval augmentation (RAG) improve usefulness and factual accuracy.

3. Examples of Generative AI tools

  • Text & chat: ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini
  • Images & art: DALL·E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion
  • Code assistance: GitHub Copilot, Tabnine
  • Audio & music: AIVA, Soundraw
  • Video & editing: Runway, Pictory

4. Strengths & limitations

Strengths:

  • Rapid prototyping and draft generation
  • Idea synthesis and summarization
  • Assistance with repetitive or boilerplate work

Limitations & risks:

  • Hallucinations — confident but incorrect outputs
  • Bias inherited from training data
  • Privacy and IP/licensing concerns
  • Overreliance can erode human expertise

5. How should I evaluate generated outputs?

Verify facts against primary sources, run code through tests and linters, check images for artifacts or misuse, and ensure outputs respect licenses and privacy rules.

6. Is Generative AI safe to use?

It can be, with safeguards: use model-specific guidance, restrict sensitive uses, validate outputs, and have human reviewers for critical decisions.
