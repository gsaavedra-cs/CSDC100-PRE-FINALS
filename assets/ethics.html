Ethical Use of Generative AI

Principles and practical guidelines for responsible use of Generative AI systems.

Core principles

  • Transparency: Document how Generative AI was used and which tools/models were involved.
  • Accountability: Keep humans in the loop and assign responsibility for final outputs.
  • Fairness: Monitor and mitigate bias; test across diverse inputs.
  • Privacy: Avoid exposing private data during prompts or training.
  • Safety: Validate outputs, especially for high-risk domains.

Governance and best practices

Organizations should implement simple, effective processes before deploying Generative AI:

  1. Impact assessment: evaluate potential harms and benefits for a given use case.
  2. Model selection and testing: choose models that support traceability and test them on representative inputs.
  3. Access controls: limit who can run generation tasks and monitor usage.
  4. Logging & provenance: record prompts, model versions, and outputs for audits.

Practical checklist for teams

  • Confirm data privacy and remove sensitive data from prompts.
  • Use RAG or citation-aware methods for factual outputs.
  • Run bias and safety tests before production rollout.
  • Provide user guidance about AI limitations and how to verify results.
